Giuseppina D'Agostino, HonBA, LLB, MSt, DPhil, Associate Professor, Osgoode Hall Law School, York University - November 23, 2023

  • Copyright protects the expression of ideas (like a book), artistic works, dramatic work and compilations of these works. The owner is the author of the work
  • The BookBuddy could fall under fair dealing which is defined as “using work for research, private study, education, satire, parody criticism, review or news reporting”
  • Whether or not you could fall under fair dealing depends on how much is being taken. The more work you are taking, the more likely it is infringement. One page should be viewed as insubstantial
  • The law is very ambiguous on the matter
  • To be cautious, she would recommend getting consent from the authors or publishers
  • With regard to the BookBuddy, there are two types of protection for computer code:
    • Copyright - easy to accomplish
    • Patent - looks at prior art; has to be something new, useful, and not obvious
