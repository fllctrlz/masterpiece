Title: “Who owns AI generated art? A primer on Canadian copyright and AI artwork”

Author(s): Hunter Parsons and Vivian Cheng

Summarized by Isaac Gabriel Cung

  • The article states, “…those who use generative AI for commercial purposes may unwittingly become entangled in copyright infringement claims.”
  • AI may be trained on copyrighted material which could be copyright infringement.
  • It can be argued that the training is temporary reproduction of copyrighted material for a technological process.
  • It can be argued that the learning processes of the AI are for research purposes under fair dealing.


Title: “Visual supports”

Author(s): The National Autistic Society

Summarized by Isaac Gabriel Cung

  • Many people with autism are visual learners.
  • There are different types of visual supports:
    • Real objects
    • Tactile symbols/objects of reference
    • Photographs
    • Miniatures of real objects
    • Coloured pictures
  • Visual supports help:
    • “Provide structure and routine” (National Autistic Society)
    • “Encourage independence” (National Autistic Society)
    • “Help reduce anxiety” (National Autistic Society)
    • “Improve people’s understanding” (National Autistic Society)
    • “Offer people opportunities to interact with other” (National Autistic Society)


Title: “Developing Visual Literacy for Greater Comprehension”

Author(s): Kathleen Ellen O’Neil

Summarized by Isaac Gabriel Cung

  • Illustrators use line, shape, colour, size, and style to make their images.
  • Four basic modes of picture/text interaction:
    • Reinforcing, images reinforce the text
    • Description, images provide support through description
    • Reciprocal, the story relies heavily on images and images help tell the story
    • Establishing, there is little text but, the image helps confirm assumptions that the reader might make



Title: “A Picture is Worth a Thousand Words: Using Visual Images to Improve Comprehension for Middle School Struggling Readers”

Author(s): Anne Nielsen Hibbing and Joan L. Rankin-Erickson

Summarized by Isaac Gabriel Cung

  • For students who struggle with reading a picture is truly worth a thousand words”. (Hibbing and Rankin-Erikson)
  • Students who create images in their head expand their “potential for understanding”. (Hibbing and Rankin-Erikson)
  • Illustrations can help students with mental imagery.
  • Illustrations can help provide context
  • Words and images can interact to develop a richer understanding of character and setting



Title: “AI tools like ChatGPT are built on mass copyright infringement”

Author(s): Zainab Choudhry

Summarized by Isaac Gabriel Cung

  • AI developers are committing mass copyright infringement by scraping all types of content.
  • Writers and artists want laws to protect them and their content from being analyzed by AI.
  • New laws for AI should be created instead of applying old laws to AI because the old laws did not have AI in mind.
  • We need to protect our intellectual property.
